About Me

Originally self-taught in programming, I am a curious, adventurous individual that never stops marveling at all there is to see and learn in the world. While I have studied and worked primarily in JavaScript/HTML/CSS, I pride myself in the fact that first and foremost I am a software engineer, computer scientist, and problem-solver, agnostic to the technology of the day. I enjoy the fun of quickly acquiring functionality in new technologies. One of the main things this industry has taught me is that no matter what you are learning, the most important aspect is understanding HOW to learn and how you personally learn.

My professional interests include full stack development, data visualizations, data science, and cellular automaton. I can regularly be found geeking out in front of my computer, and pursuing some new endeavor in a lifelong quest of education and knowledge. In my free time, I enjoy traveling the world with a camera, my laptop, and a backpack. I am an active vale tudo and MMA fighter, and I also regularly enjoy powerlifting, mountain biking, skydiving, scuba diving, and anything else involving physical and mental challenge.

You can also visit my new travel blog Forty Liters Of Adventure, or navigate/scroll down to the footer for links to my Github, LinkedIn, and FaceBook
